7
Weird Science

Solar-Powered
Animals

Costasiella kuroshimae

A tiny sea slug steals chloroplasts from algae and incorporates them into its own cells, sustaining photosynthesis for weeks — essentially becoming solar-powered. This is kleptoplasty: the animal equivalent of running a stolen power plant. The biology is genuinely strange; it's not clear how the chloroplasts keep working outside a plant cell. One of those Wikipedia rabbitholes that will recalibrate your sense of what's possible in biology.
